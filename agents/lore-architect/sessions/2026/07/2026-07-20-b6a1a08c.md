# Re-surveyed the competitive landscape and locked in a positioning triad

The session opened with the user asking whether any similar plugins or projects to the lore framework already existed, requesting a fresh internet search rather than relying on prior knowledge. The lore-architect had a landscape survey from 2026-07-02 on file (`similar-projects-landscape.md`), but 18 days is enough time for this space to move, so the request was treated as a genuine re-survey rather than a lookup.

The web sweep surfaced a striking development: three external projects are now literally named "Lore" — amarlearning/lore (Claude-Code-only, automatic hook-based capture of decision reasoning into a version-controlled `.lore/` directory, team-shared via git, nascent at 4 stars), BYK/loreai (a cross-engine LLM proxy doing three-tier distillation into `.lore.md`/`AGENTS.md`, 85 stars, very active, with a planned "Folk Lore" team-sync feature), and getlore-ai (hosted, least similar). Beyond the name collision, the survey found that claude-mem — the personal, Claude-bound tool from the July 2 survey — has gone cross-engine and grown to roughly 65K stars, and that Google announced the Open Knowledge Format (OKF) on 2026-06-12: a vendor-neutral spec for markdown-plus-frontmatter knowledge graphs in git, occupying almost exactly lore-framework's substrate space. AGENTS.md has also been standardized under the Linux Foundation. Findings were written up as a full rewrite of `similar-projects-landscape.md`, plus a new backlog item and a standing-list entry (C5) for evaluating OKF alignment — flagging the direct convention conflict that OKF requires frontmatter on every knowledge file while lore topics are deliberately frontmatter-free.

The user then asked directly: are we still competitive, is the market proposition unique, do we still have something special? The lore-architect answered that the framework retains a triad no surveyed competitor has any piece of — named role-based agents as the unit of knowledge (versus everyone else's codebase- or project-scoped memory), a deliberate skill-triggered reflect/merge curation lifecycle (versus universal automatic hook/proxy capture), and cross-agent collaboration mechanisms (attach/consult/recall/spawn-teammate) — plus the harder-to-copy asset of ~150 topics of self-hosted design lore proving the system works on itself. But two axes that anchored the July 2 positioning — "cross-engine" and "team-shared via git" — no longer differentiate, since multiple competitors now claim both. The honest risk framing: BYK/loreai's roadmapped "Folk Lore" release is a convergence event that could collapse the remaining gap, and the market currently rewards zero-friction automatic capture (claude-mem's star count) more than deliberate curation, so the framework's bet is contrarian and needs to be argued rather than assumed.

The user asked to preserve this analysis explicitly for future advertising and competitor-comparison use, and to finalize the session. The lore-architect wrote a dedicated positioning document into `workdir/positioning-analysis-2026-07-20.md` (draft pitch line, risk assessment, action hooks) ahead of running reflect, then wrote two reflection topics: one flagging a stale positioning claim to patch ("no surveyed competitor federates knowledge across different coding engines," now false) and one capturing the triad framing plus a re-survey-cadence rule and the name-collision watch item. Merge ran as a single Sonnet-model subagent per the user's request, which created a new canonical lore topic, `positioning-triad-differentiation.md`, and patched the stale claim in `multi-engine-portability-direction.md`, `lore-agents-product-name.md` (new name-collision section), `lore-context.md`, and `workdir/what-to-improve.md`'s C3 item — replacing absolutist "no competitor" language with the triad framing throughout.

The session landed with all lore changes merged and uncommitted, ready for the finalize commit. Nothing was left unresolved from the user's ask; the main open thread is the action hooks the positioning document names but doesn't execute — rewriting README/marketplace copy to lead with the triad, and raising the priority of the pending Claude community marketplace submission — both deferred to a future session as deliberate follow-ups, not oversights.
