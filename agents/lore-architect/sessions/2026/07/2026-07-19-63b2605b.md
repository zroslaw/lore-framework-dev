# Simplified Lore Beings design to a minimal MVP shape

The session opened with the user asking lore-architect to review the already-settled Lore Beings / Being Keeper design draft and suggest improvements, under an explicit directive: keep the initial design as simple as possible, and defer to the future any field, attribute, or logic not needed for the MVP ("the Chronicler lives for a week").

The review produced a ranked list of nine simplification candidates — collapsing the model-tier system down to plain `engine:`/`model:` fields, dropping the unenforced `autonomy:` schema field in favor of fixed reflect-only prose, deferring `lrb-*` skills in favor of CLI-only for the MVP, cutting budget enforcement to a daily cap plus a wall-clock timeout, dropping `timezone:`, merging three machine-level config files into one, and trimming the CLI surface — each paired with an explicit "what I deliberately did not cut" list protecting the safety substrate (the machine-wide concurrency cap, wall-clock kill, dead-man pause file, atomic state writes). On the user's go-ahead, all nine were applied directly to `workdir/draft-lore-beings.md`, with every cut preserved in a "deferred seams" section carrying its own concrete reintroduction trigger rather than being silently discarded.

A second review pass then surfaced seven operational gaps the MVP week would actually hit in practice: a missed-fire same-day-catch-up policy for laptop sleep (the most consequential finding — a closed lid at the morning-wakeup time would otherwise silently kill the day's entire self-scheduling chain), a concrete result-capture contract (the Keeper redirects engine stdout to the session log; Claude headless's final JSON *is* the result, with no separate protocol needed), an honest statement of the daily budget's overshoot bound, a 24-hour horizon on one-shot outbox requests to keep validation and the daily clock aligned, giving the Chronicler its own throwaway agent repo to make zero-blast-radius structural, clarifying that the spawn-prompt "session summary" is a log artifact rather than the `sessions/` finalization machinery, and a stub-engine convention for deterministic, zero-cost dev-repo tests. These were folded into the draft as well.
